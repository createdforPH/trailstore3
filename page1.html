Recipe: Light-Weight Hair Conditioner
12, OCTOBER, 2022

Since the reason to start my journey is my experience with a conditioner bar - here's my first recipe that would fit all kinds of hair types. I've tried it personally on my own and it worked wonderfully with my dry frizzy wavy curls. My husband Paul also tried this bar up to its last lather and we're both hooked on using it. After our experience, we no longer wanted to use commercially store-bought conditioner from the supermarket. I've decided to share this recipe with you so you can experience it on your own - or just buy it from OUR STORE !

Using ALL-NATURAL ingredients means we've used plant-based and naturally occurring extracts and minerals. Using grapeseed oil and aloevera extract is my first choice to nourish hair from super dry to oily type, it's the most basic conditioner bar for normal hair to maintain it's texture and over-all health. These power-duo protects the hair from sun damage, deeply hydrates & conditions while promoting hair growth & strengthening each strand while maintaining oil balance.

INGREDIENTS:
(heat phase)
15 % 4.94g mango butter
24 % 7.92g btms 50
2 % 0.66g beeswax
30. % 7.92g cetearyl alcohol
0.83% 0.27g allantoin
5. % 1.65g grapeseed
(cool-down phase)
11.70% 3.86g fractionated coconut oil
2.50% 0.83g aloe vera extract
2 % 0.66g rice bran oil
a pinch of. mica dye (optional)
(pre-pouring)
3 % 0.99g fragrance oil (optional)

In every procedure, I highly recommend to measure all your ingredients first and ready all your tools. I measure all my heat phase ingredients directly to a heat-resistant borosilicate glass bowl (best one is the Pyrex glass bowl). In a separate container, measure all the cool-down phase and another bowl for the fragrance oil since the scent was greatly affected by heat - it's best to mix your fragrance just a few seconds before pouring to your mold.

Using a casserole, (where your heat-resistant glass bowl can fit) ready a water bath just enough to reach the ingredients inside your glass bowl. Turn the stove on into medium heat as your heat phase ingredients melt. It would only take about 2-5 minutes before your heat phase ingredients melt. Have an infrared thermometer ready within reach to check the temperature of your mixture - take note to keep the temperature not higher than 85 degrees celsius.

Once melted, you can take out your glass bowl and place it on top of a table cloth to keep it warm or you can keep it on the stove (but make sure to keep the temperature not higher than 70 degrees celsius). Immediately pour your cool down phase ingredients and stir throughly, making sure to dissolve any lumps.

Ready your mold, I spray it with isopropyl rubbing alcohol, but this is optional.
Pour your fragrance oil to your mixture, make a quick stir and pour to your molds. You can spray the surface with isopropyl rubbing alcohol to avoid bubble formation.

To use your lightweight eco-conditioner bar, rub or apply into wet hair just like a regular conditioner. It is best to use a soap mesh like this to form more lather while keeping your bar dry in the shower when hanged.

Enjoy and experience soft and beautiful hair with every wash while saving the planet with a zero-waste conditioner bar!

SUBSTITUTIONS
You can use other butters, but I recommend shea butter which has the closest hardness and melting property of the mango butter.
Btms 50 is quite difficult to replace, you can only swap it with btms 25 which will only give you half the moisturizing property that can benefit people with oily hair.
Beeswax is a very common material and readily available everywhere. Cetearyl alcohol can be replaced with a combination of cetyl alcohol and stearic acid to form your fatty alcohol and acid at 6:4 ratio (6, cetyl alcohol: 4, stearic acid).
Allantoin is a natural source of panthenol B5, you can use your panthenol B5 or another natural source like the comfrey extract.
Grapeseed gives the lightweight moisturizing property of this bar, but you can swap it with other light oils that you prefer.
Fractionated coconut oil provides additional lather to this bar so if you're planning to replace it, try using other lathering oils like coconut or castor oil.
Aloevera extract is the one that gives this bar an exceptional hydrating feature, I highly recommend not to swap this since you can easily acquire this in the supermarket or from your garden. You can try using cucumber extract instead if you still wish to replace this extract.
Rice bran oil is the natural alternative for Vitamin E acetate, you can use your vitamin e if using synthetic raw material is not a problem for you.
Mica dye & fragrance oil are just cosmetic ingredients for a colorful and aromatic bars. If you have hypersensitive skin, I recommend you to skip this and just replace the fragrance oil with other hair-loving oils.
